The Kagu

The Bird of Praise

The Kagu (pictured above)

The Kagu is a highly unusual, flightless bird - which is known locally as the "Ghost of the Forest" (A pretty cool name).
They are also the only representative of an entire taxonomic family, as the Kagu resembles something between a small heron
and a rail. It shares its taxonomic order with one other species, which is also the only representative of its family, the sunbitten.

This species is endemic to Grand Terre, the largest island of New Caledonia and has been adopted as the national emblem.
The Kagu has experienced a sharp decline in numbers during the 1900s as a direct result of predation by invasive species.
Following this predation bydogs and cats - the Kagu is now listed as Endangered.

Map of New Caledonia

The Kagu profile:

  • Order: Eurypygiformes
  • Family: Rhynochertidae
  • Population: < 2000
  • Size: 55cm
  • Weight: 700-1100g
